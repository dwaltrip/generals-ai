# 5.17-4 Training-Spike Plan — Fresh-Look Review

**Date:** 2026-05-17
**Role:** Fresh-perspective review of [`5.17-4-training-spike-plan.md`](./5.17-4-training-spike-plan.md) (doc B) by an Opus reviewer.
**Scope:** Accuracy first, then coherence/consistency/structure/flow. Polish standard normal (this is doc B, not the scratchpad).

---

## Top-line

The plan is well-organized, the phase ordering is right, and the lock vs. defer split is largely defensible. Most accuracy issues are small but a handful are load-bearing:
- The Phase 2 sub-step that says `cities` is "monotonic from `cities_present_at <= t`" reads the parser field directly and **bypasses fog**; this is not what `5.05-1` §3.1 specifies for the persistent structure channels.
- The action-mask shape claimed in Phase 1 (`[H, W, 9]`) doesn't match the post-spike head shape locked in §4.1 (per-cell 8 + scalar pass).
- The map-padding decision (pad to 32×32) silently drops larger games (33-cell side maps exist per doc A §6); the policy on those is undefined.

Several smaller mismatches against the codebase / design docs are listed below. Cross-references inside doc B mostly work; a couple point at the wrong subsection.

## Accuracy issues

### A1. `cities` channel: "monotonic from `cities_present_at <= t`" bypasses fog [load-bearing]

§3 Phase 2 step 1, fog-computation block:
> **Persistent structure channels:** `mountains` (static), `cities` (monotonic from `cities_present_at <= t`), `generals` (monotonic; cleared on capture/neutralize via `capture_events` / `neutralize_events`).

`cities_present_at` is a parser-side field that records when each city *exists on the board*, not when the perspective player *discovered it*. Indexing `cities[cities_present_at <= t]` (per 5.16-3 §2.2) gives the full set of cities present at time `t` — including cities the perspective has never seen.

The obs-tensor spec (`5.05-1` §3.1) explicitly redefines `cities` to be persistent *knowledge*: "1 for any cell *known* to be a city (currently visible OR previously explored). Once set, never unset." Same redefinition for `mountains` and `generals`. So `cities` (and `generals`, and to a lesser extent `mountains`-in-fog) must be derived from the perspective's vision walk, not from the raw parser fields.

For the spike, you could intentionally simplify and feed all city positions upfront — but that's a deviation from the obs spec the rest of the plan claims to inherit, and it should be called out as such. As written, this row reads as a faithful inheritance from `5.05-1` when in fact it short-circuits the discovery semantics.

(`generals` mostly works as stated because the dataloader will know generals only after first sighting, but the row mixes the parser-derived approach for cities with the vision-derived approach for generals without flagging the inconsistency.)

### A2. Phase 1 vs Phase 2 action-space shape inconsistency [load-bearing]

Phase 1 step 5 (skeleton dataloader emission):
> **Action mask:** `[H, W, 9]` boolean of legal moves.

§4.1 row "Policy head" and Phase 2 step 8:
> H×W×8 (4 dir × 2 split) + scalar pass logit

These disagree on the per-cell channel count (9 vs. 8) because the locked design splits pass out into a scalar. Phase 1's `[H, W, 9]` is the legacy `network-architecture-design.md` §4 shape; the spike decides against it. Either:
- Make Phase 1 emit `[H, W, 8] + scalar` from the start (cleaner, since you're writing the smoke test anyway), or
- Explicitly note that Phase 1's `[H, W, 9]` is placeholder until Phase 2 step 8 reshapes it.

The Phase 1 exit criterion at line 88 — "mask covers ≥1 legal cell per non-pass frame" — also assumes the 9-channel form; with pass split out it becomes "for non-pass frames, the per-cell mask covers ≥1 legal cell."

### A3. Map padding policy is silent on >32-cell-side games [load-bearing]

§3 Phase 2 step 4 + §4.1 row "Map handling" lock 32×32 padding. Doc A §6 noted "24–33 cell side lengths" and §4.4.3 explicitly framed 32×32 as covering "90% of FFA games" — implying 10% are larger. Doc B doesn't specify what to do with 33-cell-side games. Three reasonable options:

- Filter them out at dataloader time (loses ~10% of training samples).
- Bump the pad to 34 or 36 (small compute cost; matches doc A §4.4.3's framing more honestly).
- Confirm via a 5-minute corpus sweep that the actual large-side rate is small enough to drop without notice.

As written the plan will silently throw on an oversized map at runtime — which is debuggable but not the kind of surprise Phase 3 should be discovering.

### A4. §4.1 "Training-time skill filter" row drops a clause

> Training-time skill filter | None (use parser's noise floor as-is) | Parser already pre-filters at `prior_games ≥ 50 ∧ 1st-rate > 12.5%`.

Driver code (`replay-parser/replay_parser/driver.py:48–58, 179–183`) applies three conditions: `prior_games ≥ 50 ∧ 1st-rate > 12.5% ∧ top-3 > 37.5%`. §2.1's identical sentence has all three; §4.1's row truncates to the first two. Round-trip fix.

### A5. Cross-reference: §3 Phase 2 step 5 "see §4.3 for why we split this out" points at the wrong subsection

Step 5 line:
> For pass: a separate scalar pass logit (see §4.3 for why we split this out).

§4.3 is the action-encoding round-trip — it does not explain *why* pass is split out. The rationale is in Phase 2 step 8 (line 125: "Why split pass out: 'per-cell pass' is semantically odd, and the pass logit can carry its own gradient signal..."). Either move the rationale earlier or update the cross-reference.

### A6. "Cleared on capture/neutralize via `capture_events` / `neutralize_events`" — minor mechanic gap

§3 Phase 2 step 1 says `generals` is "monotonic; cleared on capture/neutralize via `capture_events` / `neutralize_events`."

Per `5.05-1` §3.1 and the parser, when a general is captured/AFK'd, the general tile *becomes a city* — and `cities_present_at` records that. So "cleared from `generals`" + "added to `cities`" is the right transition pair. Doc B's row only mentions the clear half. Easy to miss in implementation if the dataloader doesn't pick up the city-add half from `cities_present_at`. Minor; flagging.

### A7. `actions_is50` sentinel description in §2.1 is fine; cross-check the encoding

§2.1 is correct: `actions_is50` uses `-1` for pass (the `int8` arrays accommodate the sentinel; `0`/`1` retain wire meaning). §4.3 `encode()` only checks `source_idx == -1` for the pass branch, which matches — the three arrays are pass-or-not in lockstep per 5.16-3 §2.3. No issue, just verifying.

### A8. Distance-from-known-generals: fogged-cell traversal rule under-specified

§3 Phase 2 step 1 says: "BFS on the known-passable graph per general; log-scaled; -1 sentinel for unreachable or unrevealed."

The obs-tensor spec (`observation-tensor-design.md` §2.1, "BFS scope") adds two more constraints:
- Fogged cells treated as opaque (not traversable for this BFS — agent plans on what it knows, not optimistic assumptions).
- Cities are passable, uniform cost for v1.

The "opaque under fog" rule is the load-bearing one; without it the channel computes optimistic distances through unexplored terrain. Doc B's "known-passable graph" implies it but doesn't state it. Worth a one-line clarification.

### A9. Exit criterion "1/9 → ~11% per-direction" mixes action-space shapes

§3 Phase 3 exit criterion:
> top-k accuracy beats random (1/9 → ~11% per-direction; with action masking, probably much higher).

The "1/9" is the network-arch §4 per-cell 9-channel shape; the spike's policy is per-cell 8 + scalar pass (= 65 classes over a 1024-cell padded board pre-mask). The intent — "post-mask accuracy is meaningfully better than uniform over legal options" — is fine, but the numeric baseline as stated doesn't match the locked action space.

### A10. §1 "165k games" framing matches disk

Verified via `find replay-parser/data/intermediate -name "*.meta.npz" | wc -l` = 165,608 (and 165,608 sim files). Both `165k` and `9.2 GB` are accurate. Curated-list manifest sums to 334 names (132 + 50 + 45 + 107) across the 4 files — matches §2.1.

### A11. Schema-drift note in §2.1 is accurate

Cross-checked `replay-parser/replay_parser/metadata.py:46–48`: `sim_core_version` is `<U24`, `perspective_usernames` is `<U32 [K]`. Both reflect current code; the design-time 5.16-3 §4.1 lists `<U16` and lacks `perspective_usernames`. Doc B's "treat the code as canonical" framing is the right call.

### A12. Phase 3 step 6 "model + parser git SHAs"

Phase 3 step 6 says: "Capture: hyperparams, train/val loss per batch + epoch, top-1 + top-3 action accuracy, action-distribution histogram, pass-fraction, model + parser git SHAs."

"Parser git SHA" already lives in the meta sidecar's `sim_core_version` (which despite the name is the repo SHA — driver.py captures the full repo state, not just `sim-core/`). Doc B should clarify whether it's pulling that field from each loaded meta file (and asserting all loaded games match) or storing a single SHA per run; the latter is sufficient since the per-game SHA is already in the sidecar. Minor; mainly to avoid double-bookkeeping.

## Coherence / consistency issues

### CO1. Phase 2 step 6 (pass-rate sweep) is mis-located in the dependency graph

Step 6 says "Before locking the loss" — but step 10 *locks the loss*. The ordering within Phase 2 is therefore correct (6 before 10). However:

- Step 6 is also called a "30-minute `np.load` pass" — pure data inspection. It has zero dependency on Phase 1 or any earlier Phase 2 step except "decided we care about pass rate." It is a Phase 1 task (or even pre-Phase-1) by dependency order. Currently sitting between action-encoding (step 5) and model code (step 7), it disrupts the otherwise-clean flow of Phase 2 ("build the model, run a step").
- The §4.2 "empirically resolved" table also lists this check as the first item; the table and the phase ordering tell slightly different stories about where it belongs.

Suggest: move the sweep to Phase 1 (alongside the MPS benchmark — both are "cheap data + env probes you do before you commit to the architecture") or split it into its own §0.5 / pre-Phase-1 prep step. Phase 2 then reads as "build model, train one batch."

### CO2. §4.1 "Slot canonicalization" row contradicts §3 Phase 3 step 3's framing of "implicit randomness"

§4.1 row: "Slot canonicalization | Dataloader-side rotation; perspective always at slot 0 | Parser keeps natural ordering; rotation key is `perspective_player_ids`."

§3 Phase 3 step 3: "Slot permutation deferred for v1 spike — defensible because (a) canonical-by-ID slot ordering already gets implicit randomness from generals.io's unstructured slot assignment (5.05-3 §3.3)..."

These are not contradictory — canonicalization (rotate perspective to slot 0) is distinct from slot-permutation augmentation (randomize the opponent-slot ordering). But the §4.1 row collapses them into "rotation" without distinguishing, and a fresh reader is going to conflate them. Worth one sentence: "Canonicalization places perspective at slot 0; opponents stay in their natural (generals.io-assigned) order across slots 1..P-1. The full slot-permutation augmentation that randomly shuffles those opponent slots is deferred — see §4.4."

Also, §3 Phase 3 step 3's "(a)" rationale leans on the assumption that generals.io's slot assignment is effectively random, which `5.05-3` §3.3 explicitly flags as an open assumption that was never spot-checked ("the original plan was to spot-check this in replay data before next session; that check was subsequently folded into future dataset prep / parser work"). Doc B is treating an unverified premise as load-bearing. The conclusion is probably right (defer slot-perm-aug is fine for a spike), but the framing should not present this as settled.

### CO3. Phase 2 step 1 "Vision mask" wording — minor terminology

> the union of the Moore neighborhood (8 surrounding cells + the cell itself) around every cell owned by the perspective player at this snapshot.

`generals-io-game-mechanics.md` line 81 specifies Moore-neighborhood (the 8 surrounding cells) around every owned tile. The "+ the cell itself" is colloquially right (you obviously see what you own) but isn't part of the formal Moore neighborhood. Either drop the parenthetical or rephrase "Moore neighborhood plus the owned cell itself." Minor.

### CO4. §3 Phase 1 step 5 "11 channels" placeholder vs. Phase 2 "89 channels"

Phase 1 emits 11 placeholder channels (ownership-one-hot 8 + log-armies 1 + mountains 1 + cities 1). Phase 2 jumps to 89. The transition is large and isn't covered by an intermediate verification step — Phase 2 starts with full fog computation. Reasonable, but worth saying "Phase 2 fully replaces the Phase 1 channel set" so the reader doesn't expect an incremental swap.

Also: the Phase 1 ownership one-hot uses 8 channels but the parser stores 4 distinct values for ownership: `-2` (mountain), `-1` (neutral), `0..P-1` (player). A one-hot-per-slot encoding (8 channels) skips neutrals entirely unless mountains channel + cities channel cover it implicitly. Fine for a placeholder but slightly under-described.

### CO5. §3 Phase 3 step 5's "Per epoch end: evaluate on val split; log to JSONL; save checkpoint" pairs with §3 Phase 4 step 1

Phase 4 step 1 says "already in phase 3's per-epoch eval." The Phase 4 deliverable then mostly repackages metrics that were already being computed. This is fine but reads as "Phase 4 isn't a phase, it's the eval pass *of* Phase 3." Two minor implications:
- Could merge Phase 4 step 1 into Phase 3 step 5 or call out the redundancy.
- Phase 4 effort estimate in §6 (4–8 hours + user review) should perhaps subtract the per-epoch metric work from Phase 3.

Not a blocker; just an organizational artifact worth noticing.

### CO6. §4.2 table vs. Phase numbering

§4.2's "Affects" column for the first row says "Pass-head loss weighting (§3 phase 2 step 6)." That points to the same step that *is* the sweep (step 6), not what the sweep affects (which is step 10's loss formulation). Minor wording fix: should be "§3 phase 2 step 10 loss weighting" or similar.

## Lower-priority items

### L1. "Phase 1 step 3 ≥5× MPS speedup" is an arbitrary threshold

The MPS-vs-CPU bar of 5× isn't anchored in any inherited decision. Could be 3×, could be 10×. For a spike, "enough that the runs are pleasant" is the real criterion. Minor; not blocking. Suggests phrasing like "MPS shows enough headroom over CPU that an iteration loop is pleasant" instead of a fake-precise multiplier.

### L2. §7 "What the spike won't answer" is well-calibrated

The five-bullet list of "you won't know after the spike" matches the framing in §1's "don't need" list. Good expectation-setting; nothing to change.

### L3. §8 open questions and §4.4 deferred — minor overlap

§8 items 1 and 2 (drop `opp_N_has_seen`, binary-vs-categorical value head) are also in §4.4 (deferred to post-spike: "The `opp_N_has_seen` channels — empirical check..." and "Auxiliary heads"... well, that's different from binary-vs-categorical). Could collapse if you want; not bothersome as is.

### L4. §5 risks row "Schema relock surprises"

The wording "code is canonical; rewrites are cheap (~6 min)" matches the 5.17-1 framing. Note for doc B: the ~6 min number is the **full corpus re-write** time on 8 workers (per 5.17-1). It's accurate as cited but the row implies "schema rewrite is cheap" — which is true for the *corpus rewrite*, less true for any downstream code that hardcodes shapes. Worth a half-sentence to make explicit.

### L5. §3 Phase 2 step 7 ResBlock structure

The "C → C/2 → C with 1×1 residual on stride. N=2 outer, M=2 middle, M=2 inner" matches network-arch §3.3 + §4. Verified accurate.

### L6. §3 Phase 2 step 11 optimizer

"AdamW, lr=3e-4, weight_decay=1e-4, betas=(0.9, 0.999). No warmup, no decay schedule" — network-arch §3.3 specifies wd=1e-4 explicitly, leaves lr / betas / warmup open. Doc B is committing past the inherited spec, which is fine for spike-time locking, but the phrasing "Network-arch §3.3 defaults, locked" in §4.1 over-attributes — `lr=3e-4` and `betas=(0.9, 0.999)` are the *standard Adam* defaults, not the network-arch's. Minor framing fix: "AdamW with network-arch §3.3's wd=1e-4 + standard lr/betas/warmup defaults."

### L7. §5 risk "66 `skip:exception`" action item is concrete

Good — names the action (CSV-parse + `type(e).__name__` count) and the criterion (defer unless structural). This is what doc A's review asked for and doc B delivered.

### L8. §3 Phase 3 step 2 wall-clock napkin math

5.5M samples / 64 batch = 86k batches. At 5 ms/batch → 430 sec ≈ 7 min. At 10 ms/batch → 14 min. Doc says "~6 minutes/epoch raw" — that's the optimistic end of the range. The "3–5× slower → ~20–30 min/epoch" then gives ~21–70 min depending on which raw number you take. The 3–5 epochs/evening claim is plausible across the range. Not wrong; just sensitive to which raw number you anchor.

### L9. Read-Clean Check pass

Doc B mostly reads forward-looking and free of "previously X, now Y" framing that doesn't belong. One section that could tighten: §2.1's schema-drift bullet leans on "shipped with `<U24` (not `<U16` per 5.16-3 §4.1, to fit the `-dirty` suffix)" — this is the kind of "look at the journey" framing that belongs in a session note, not necessarily a forward-looking plan. The same fact could be one sentence: "Meta sidecar stamp uses `<U24` (room for `-dirty` suffix) and includes `perspective_usernames <U32 [K]`. Treat the shipped code as canonical." Marginally tighter.

## Summary of what's worth fixing before this plan goes operational

In rough priority:

1. **`cities` channel under fog** (A1): the Phase 2 step 1 wording bypasses fog. Either change the rule or call out the simplification explicitly.
2. **Action-mask / action-space shape**: Phase 1's `[H, W, 9]` doesn't match §4.1's `[H, W, 8] + scalar pass`. Pick one (recommend the latter from the start).
3. **Map-padding policy on >32-side games** (A3): silently breaks at runtime as written. Pick a rule (drop / pad bigger / verify rate).
4. **Re-locate the pass-rate sweep** (CO1): currently buried mid-Phase 2. It's a Phase-1-class cheap probe; moving it up tidies the flow and makes its result available before the loss is wired.
5. **§4.1 noise-floor row** (A4): missing the `top-3 > 37.5%` clause that §2.1 has correctly.
6. **Cross-ref §3 Phase 2 step 5 → §4.3** (A5): broken pointer; the rationale lives in step 8.
7. **`5.05-3` §3.3 "implicit randomness" framing** (CO2): an unverified assumption; either flag it or pick a more conservative rationale for deferring slot-perm augmentation.
8. **§4.1 slot-canonicalization row vs. slot-permutation augmentation** (CO2): collapsing two distinct concepts into one row that reads as both at once.

Smaller items (terminology, cross-refs, optimizer attribution, channel-count nits, etc.) are listed above; none blocks the spike.

The plan's biggest strength is the lock-table in §4.1 — once the issues above are fixed, the spike has a clean "go" gate. The fog-computation block in Phase 2 step 1 is where the real implementation risk lives (per the plan's own §5 risk #3), and that block deserves the most scrutiny in the next pass.
