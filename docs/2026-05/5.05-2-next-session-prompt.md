# Background

I'm designing the neural network architecture for for an 8-player FFA [generals.io](http://generals.io) AI agent. In brief, we are aiming for pure behavioral cloning on a large corpus of expert replays,  with a conv-based U-Net architecture that has a strong basis in the prior art.

Our main focus recently has been the observation tensor:

* Two sessions ago, we systematically designed the majority of the the input channels, except long-term memory, and produced a detailed "partial spec" documenting our work.
* Our most recent session finished a "first draft" of the entire input tensor, including the missing long-term memory. We also produced another detailed doc.

The rough high-level strategy so far has been to borrow as much as possible from the two major pieces of prior art:

1. The Straka and Schmdit "generals.io 1v1" agent. Referred to in our docs as "strakam" or "generals 1v1" paper / bot / agent / etc.
2. The  Perolat et al. Stratego agent, which was the key architectural inspiration for strakam. Referred to as "Perolat", "Deepnash", or "Stratego" paper / bot / agent / etc.
